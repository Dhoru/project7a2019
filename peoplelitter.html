Why Do People Litter?

Muhammad Ammar Faisal, March 31, 2019

Litter on a street (above)

Littering continues to be a big environmental problem in cities.

According to the California State University, the farther away you are from a trash can or a recycling container, the more likely you are to litter. People are also more likely to litter if they are in a place that's already highly littered, than a place that is clean.

The presence of existing litter was strongly predictive of littering behavior.

However, in Male', people are ignorant about littering and there are not enough garbage cans on the streets. Littering is a common practice because the streets are littered.

So, maybe if cities put trash or recycling containers in public spaces, maybe people start doing the right thing again.

Also, if people stop littering entirely, other people will also not litter and the place will remain clean.
